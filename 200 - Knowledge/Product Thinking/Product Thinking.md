# 🧠 Product Thinking

---

## Core Principles I Build By

### Serve the underserved
The Muslim market is massive and underserved.
Generic apps ignore this audience. Specificity wins.

### Recurring revenue over one-time
Build subscriptions, not one-time purchases.
Compounding revenue is the goal.

### Free tier must be genuinely useful
A crippled free tier destroys trust.
Free should be good. Premium should be exceptional.

### Speed over perfection
Ship clean and fast. Iterate after.
Real orders and real users beat perfect code.

### Products must feel premium
Not expensive — premium.
Dark, intentional, minimal, well-considered.
Users feel the difference even if they can't explain it.

### Self-demonstrating products win
If you need a long explanation, rethink the product.
Sim Racing Controller: just show gameplay footage.
Nafs: just show the UI and the insight report.

---

## The Solo Builder Advantage
- No meetings, no consensus, no politics
- Ship faster than teams
- Closer to the user — you ARE the user
- AI-assisted development removes the coding barrier
- Weakness: bandwidth — prioritize ruthlessly

---

## Prioritization Rule
One primary project at a time.
Everything else is planning mode.
Split focus = slow everything down.

---

## What Makes A Product Last
- Solves a real pain, not an imagined one
- Has a natural distribution channel built in
- Recurring revenue potential
- Can be built and maintained solo
- Compounds — gets better the more people use it
